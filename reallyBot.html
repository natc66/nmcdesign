The Overview

November - December 2016
Completed for Intro to Python Class (HCDE 310)
Role: Developer

The Problem

During my Research on Women in the Super Smash Brothers Community, I learned that many women feel uncomfortable on online platforms because of negative and toxic comments. Specifically, many women pointed to the popular online streaming platform, Twitch.

The current ways that Twitch deals with negativity and toxicity, is moderators, and allowing streamers to ban words. Allowing streamers to ban words on their channels only goes so far and there are a couple issues with moderators. Moderators are often just friends of the streamer, and don't have a lot of resources to look at or training. It's also difficult to keep up when there are a lot of people in the chat.

The Solution

I created a bot is able to connect to a Twitch channel and moderate the chat by monitoring the chat messages and sending them through the google natural language API. It then stores the information of the user if the message is determined to be negative. If the user reaches 5, 10, or 15 negative messages, they are timed out for 10 minutes. If the user reaches 20 negative messages, they are banned from the channel.

The Process

Over one month I went through ideation, coding, and presenting.

Ideation

I learned about sentiment analysis API's during a hackathon I previously attended and created Myndful. That got me thinking about how that could be used to filter out negative comments. I decided I wanted to combine the google natural language API (sentiment analysis) and the Twitch.tv api.

Coding

The first step was connecting to the Twitch server and receiving data from a specific channel. One problem I ran into was making sure the channel name was spelled correctly and had a # in front of the name. This problem stumped me for awhile and I had to ask a friend for help on identifying the problem.

The second step was connecting to the google natural language API. This proved to be difficult as I found many different instructions on how to connect. Eventually I worked out how to connect by using a variety of different instructions.

The third step was to take the messages from the Twitch channel and feed them through the natural language API. The problem with this was I had to account for messages from the server that were not chat messages such as command messages.

Finally, I had to send a command to Twitch chat to ban or time out a user. This was not too difficult as there were many resources online.

The Presentation

Reflection

I completed a prototype that had all of the functionalities which I'm very proud of. I'm not the best at programming and this was my first experience with python. The code is definitely messy and unorganized, but hey! it works.

If I was to do this project over I would try to have the code be more organized. I'd also want to create all of the functionalities I initially planned on creating. For example, I wanted the bot to be able to private message people to let them know why they were timed out or banned.
