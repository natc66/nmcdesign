Transforming Couch

The Overview

December 2014 - February 2015;
May - June 2015;
November - December 2017
Completed for self driven project, 3D art and design class (ART 112 BC), Intro to visualization and CAD (ME 123 UW)
Role: Industrial Designer

The Problem

There are many people who live in small living spaces. It may be because of where they live, for example crowded cities, or because they are low income and cannot afford larger spaces. Focusing on those who are low income, it may be difficult for them to buy multiple pieces of furniture. A current solution for this is transforming furniture or space saving furniture that already exists. However, these can often be pricey and hard to come by.

The Solution

I created a piece of furniture that can move between a couch and a table with a bench. The backrest of the couch slides upwards and rests on the tops of the armrests while the seat of the couch slides out and becomes a bench.

The ability to transform this piece saves space in a room and uses less materials. This keeps both space used and cost down.

I created three different prototypes to display my ideas, including;

  • Sketches
  • A Physical Model
  • A 3D CAD Model

The Process

There were four distinct phases of this project; ideation, final sketches, a physical model, and a CAD model.

Ideation

Before starting this project I had been interested in designing a couch that resembled ocean waves. However, I decided against this because it did not solve a problem. Transforming furniture had fascinated me for a long time and I wanted to experiment with that idea.

I focused on a couch and a table because they are both large pieces of furniture that occupy a lot of space. They are also often found in the same rooms or rooms that are close to each other. (dining & living room, kitchen & family room).

I began sketching different ideas about how to create a couch and table in one. While sketching, I also researched standard measurements of tables, chairs, and couches to evaluate the feasibility of my ideas.

Sketches

I hadn't been satisfied with any of my previous ideas. This was because it would have been very difficult to get the measurements to work correctly, so that it would be comfortable for the user.

After working with the problem more, I came up with the idea to have the back of the couch slide upwards and rest on the armrests to create a table. I thought that this idea was promising so I ran with it.

One problem I ran into with this idea was that in order to have a comfortable amount of space between the table and the bench, the armrests had to be much higher than a normal couch. In order to accommodate this increase in height, I added a ledge where the armrest would usually stop, and then continued the armrest up.

After I had resolved any issues I thought of and decided on a few basic measurements, I sketched out how the couch and table would look and work. I used 5 separate drawings to show the progression of the transformation, and to give different views (front & back). After I had fully sketched the piece out, I added measurements.

Physical Model

After completing the detailed sketches, I took a class about 3D Art & Design. For one of the projects, I created a model based off of the sketches. I helped with the construction of the model, however my father did the majority of the woodwork.

This project was made of scrap wood and fabric. Because the model was created from scraps, we were not able to use exact measurements. The models measurements were estimated and based upon the other measurements used in the model rather than the measurements in the sketches.

CAD Model

After completing model, I later took a class about Computer Aided Design, specifically focused on the program solidworks. For my final project, I recreated my transforming couch in solidworks. I made 8 separate parts including;

  • Left Arm
  • Right Arm
  • Bench Base
  • Bench Lid
  • Back Bottom Section
  • Back Top Section
  • Hinge
  • Caster Ball

I then put these pieces into an assembly. There were issues with having moving parts so I locked the parts in place which means that the CAD model only shows the couch.

Reflection

This was my first design project, so I learned a lot and also messed up a lot. I found it was a lot more satisfying to solve a problem rather than just making something beautiful like I had initially planned.

All of the issues I had during the ideation and sketching phase, I resolved. I started to have more problems during the physical model and CAD model phases that stemmed from the previous phases or were caused by limitations.

While creating the physical model, I ran into issues concerning the dovetail joints. The dovetail joints are how the back of the sofa stays attached to the arms but can still slide up and down. However, on the physical model, the dovetail joints were very resistant to sliding. They worked, but not well. I need to come up with an alternative sliding mechanism, however, I have limited technical knowledge and I did not have access to others knowledge as I worked alone on this project. In the future I'd like to be able to consult others with different sets of knowledge.

When creating the CAD model, the measurements I had created during sketching were not detailed enough to fully define the model. This was because, when I initially created the sketches, I did not have the experience to know what measurements were needed and which ones weren't. This resulted in creating additional measurements when creating the CAD model. If I were to repeat this project, I'd like to create all the measurements needed in the initial sketching phase.

There are also a couple issues that I have thought of since completing the different phases. One of these is that there is the possibility that the bench will roll out when someone is sitting on it in the couch position. A solution to this would be to somehow lock it into place while the piece is a couch.

The physical prototype was limited by the material I was working with. Because it was scrap wood, I didn't have as much control over the measurements as I would have liked. If I were to do it over, I would like to have created the model based on the measurements in my sketches.
