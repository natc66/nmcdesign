Women in the Super Smash Brothers Community

The Overview

March - June 2016
Completed for User Research class (HCDE 313)
Role: Researcher

The Problem

Super Smash Brothers (SSB) is a fighting game series made up of four different games played both casually and competitively. There is a community that now surrounds the competitive play of these games. However, there are less women than men in the community. I was interested in looking into why women join, stay, and/or avoid the SSB gaming community in order to determine strategies for recruitment and retention.

The Solution

The lack of women in the SSB community, or any community, hurts the community because the lack of diversity slows the distribution of new knowledge and viewpoints. A monotonous community often has similar viewpoints. Particularly for the SSB community, this results in sexist behavior and views, and lack of new play styles. The three key findings throughout all of my research include;

  • How women join or learn about the community
  • Retainment of women in the community
  • Sexism in the community

Women tend to join or learn about the community through others, mainly male friends or significant others, and recently, through media such as videos, streams, or smashboards.

Women tend to stay in the community because of competitive drive, a love of the game, and the relationships formed within the community.

Finally, women may avoid the community because of the gender ratio and sexism (e.g. microaggressions, online toxicity, and the stigma of women in gaming).

The Process

Over ten weeks I completed three field research sessions, 13 interviews, and a survey with 50 respondents.

Field Research

I conducted three field research sessions. I attended two club meetings at the University of Washington Smash club and a regional tournament called Emerald City.

During these sessions I took notes on my phone and wandered around the event. I took notes and observed for about 30 minutes during a session. After that I interacted normally with other people at the event, not in a research capacity.

Interviews

For recruitment I used social media, specifically twitter and facebook. On twitter I contacted a woman who is well known in the SSB community and she was able to advertise for me. I also used a facebook group that was dedicated for women, trans, and non binary individuals in the SSB community.

I conducted interviews mainly over skype because individuals were not located in Seattle WA. This allowed me to gain a very diverse group of participants but may have inhibited learnings because of the lack of face to face contact.

Because of time constraints, I only analyzed and used 8 of the 13 interviews I conducted in my interview report.

Survey

I implemented a survey via twitter and facebook. Again, the target demographic was women in the SSB community. I asked multiple questions that had developed from my previous research in field observations and interviews. Specifically, I asked about

  • How they were introduced to the community
  • What their perceived roles in the community are
  • Why they stay or why the didn't stay in the community
  • What issues with harassment and sexism they face
  • How they perceive women inside and outside of the community

Reflection

I enjoyed this project because of the subject matter. I am, and have been, a part of multiple gaming communities including the SSB community. There is a possibility that I allowed my own bias to come into my research, however, I attempted to base all of my findings in my observations, interviews, or surveys. My research was also impacted by the timeline of a quarter. I would have liked to take more time to go more in depth in my research, analysis, and findings.
